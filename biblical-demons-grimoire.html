Grimoire of Biblical Demons

A catalogue of named adversaries, watchers, and unclean powers in Scripture and adjacent tradition

MS. TERRA-GRM-001 · Compiled from canonical, deuterocanonical, and pseudepigraphic sources
Index · 0 entries
Scholarly note: This grimoire lists figures explicitly named or clearly personified as demonic adversaries in biblical texts and closely related Jewish/Christian writings. Later medieval grimoires (Solomon, Goetia, etc.) merged these names with unrelated spirits — those post-biblical attributions are noted where relevant but are not treated as scriptural fact. Translations vary (e.g. Isaiah 34:14 lilit vs. "night bird").
